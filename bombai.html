🏆 Why Bombai is Special

  • Commands the early market segment by ripening significantly ahead of mid-season cultivars.
  • Displays a unique green skin that turns into a beautiful yellow-orange hue with a prominent red blush at the shoulders when ripe.
  • Highly valued across North Bihar orchards for its strong institutional historical legacy and premium market returns.
  • Possesses excellent pulp-to-stone ratios with a characteristic rich, heavy fruit perfume.
  • Exhibits dependable vegetative growth well-suited for traditional agro-climatic conditions.

📊 Expert Summary

Bombai (frequently grouped under regional Malda classifications) stands as a foundational early-season commercial pillar of Bihar's mango ecosystem. Its signature capacity to yield high-quality fruit by late May safeguards growers against later monsoonal pest pressures. Characterized by vigorous open canopies and aromatic orange pulp, it remains a heavily researched benchmark for local indigenous crop preservation.
